  9. *Numbered Balls
    • Purpose

      • Keeping their eye on the ball.


    • Equipment
      1. Two participants
      2. Bat
      3. Balls w/ numbers written on them
      4. Batting helmet
      5. Screen (Optional)


    • Organization

      • The batter stands in either an imaginary batters box with a plate, or at home. The batter will face either a screen, approximately 10 to 12 feet from the batter, and a tosser alongside them, in a position to toss the ball infront of the plate.


    • Execution

      • A player or coach soft tosses a ball to the batter, who will hit the ball into the screen. The batter must call out the number on the ball before they are allowed to make contact.


    • Variations
      1. The tosser can add spin to the ball when they toss it.
      2. The tosser can toss multiple balls and call out the number the player must hit.
      3. Change the direction that the toss is made from (side, front, behind).

    • Coaching Points

      • None

  2. *Snap Backs
    • Purpose

      • Develop quick hands, increase bat speed, and forearm and wrist strength.


    • Equipment
      1. Two participants
      2. Bat
      3. Batting helmet
      4. Screen
      5. Bucket of balls


    • Organization

      • The participants will face eachother approximately 6 feet apart, one behind the screen w/ a bucket of balls while the other stands in their hitting stance.


    • Execution

      • The player behind the screen will toss a ball to the batter in rapid succession. The hitter, after making contact, will snap back into position to hit the next ball being delivered by the tosser. This process repeats until the tosser runs out of balls.


    • Variations
      1. The tosser can increase the time between pitches, allowing the batter more time to get into position.
      2. Change the types of balls thrown in each throw to simulate different pitch types.

    • Coaching Points

      • It is important to emphasize a quick movement from hands, stride, swing. Change the speed based on your players' skill level.



  3. *Barrel Out Snap Backs
    • Purpose

      • Develop quick hands to the ball, create muscle memory of batting stance, and forearm and wrist strenght


    • Equipment
      1. Two participants
      2. Bat
      3. Batting helmet
      4. Screen
      5. Bucket of balls


    • Organization

      • The participants will face eachother approximately 8 feet apart, one behind the screen w/ a bucket of balls while the other stands in their hitting stance.


    • Execution

      • The player behind the screen will toss a ball towards the batter. After the swing is completed, the hitter will extend the barrel of the bat towards their partner, behind the net. This will signal to the partner to toss the next ball. The batter will snap into their regular hitting position, drawing the bat backwards and shifting their weight, and then immediately attempt to make contact with the tossed ball. This process repeats until the tosser runs out of balls.


    • Variations

      1. The tosser can increase the time between pitches, allowing the batter more time to get into position.
      2. Change the types of balls thrown in each throw to simulate different pitch types.
      3. Changing the weight of the bat
      4. Instead of using a bat, use a hit stick to work on coordination and accuruacy

    • Coaching Points

      • It is important to emphasize a quick movement from hands, stride, swing. Change the speed based on your players' skill level. If players are having difficulty and breaking their form, regress to regular Snap Backs or increase the time between tosses.

  1. Horse
    • Purpose

      • Placing hits, determining hitter strengths and weaknesses, and consistency in hitting

    • Equipment
      • Bat bags
      • Bat
      • Bucket of balls
      • Cones
      • Standups if available


    • Organization

      • Players could be broken into teams, pairs, or play as individuals. If broken into teams, attempt to put a variety of skill levels on each team as to provide an opportunity for success each group. It will be the coaches determination if the team tosses to themselves or if they must receive tosses from the other team. If a determination cannot be made, the coach and toss. Depending on the number of balls available, players from both teams may rotate from a line behind home plate, hitter, tosser, and shagger.

      Items such as bat bags, buckets, or cones are placed randomly around the field. Stagger items at various distances and regions of the field. Be sure to remove any breakable items from the bat bags before placing them on the field. These will be the items that players attempt to hit with a ball. The coach will determine if a direct hit is necessary or what the appropriate distance to the item will be for a point.
